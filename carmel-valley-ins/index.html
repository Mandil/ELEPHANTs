The Retirement Gap

During the 1990s and 2000s companies have largely replaced pension plans with 401(k)’s. This is a BIG PROBLEM. Pensions are mandatory, 401(k)’s are voluntary. Many people do not participate and of those who are participating many do not set aside enough money in order to retire one day.

Those who do save enough often bungle their investment choices. Those who choose well pay higher investment fees generally than pension funds do. Even participants in the best-run, lowest-cost retirement funds face the risk that the market will tank — as it has done in 2000, 2001, 2002, and 2008 — when they're close to retirement. At retirement comes another issue: pensions insure against the risk that you'll outlive your money, because they pay until you die; 401(k)s don't.
